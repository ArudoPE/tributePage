Los Mirlos

Legendary Peruvian Psychedelic Cumbia group

Los Mirlos are the creators of the Amazonian cumbia, one of the many manifestations of the Peruvian cumbia. It is a group with a long history, originally from the city of Moyobamba, in the department of San Martín, whose jungle origins earned them the nickname "Los Charapas de Oro". Its beginnings date back to 1968 when they were called Los Saetas.


Thus, the founding members of the group are:


  • Jorge Rodríguez Grández (lead voice)
  • Danny Fardy Johnston (guitar)
  • Gilberto Reátegui (guitar)
  • Tony Wagner Grández (bongo)
  • Carlos Vásquez (graves)
  • Hugo Jáuregui (timpani)
  • Manuel Linares (bass)
  • Gilberto Chamorro (bass)
  • Second Gustavo Rodríguez (güiro)
  • Carlos Rodriguez
